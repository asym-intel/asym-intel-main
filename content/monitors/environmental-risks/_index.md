The Global Environmental Risks & Planetary Boundaries Monitor (GERP Monitor) tracks Earth system processes as a strategic intelligence domain. It covers planetary boundary transgression, tipping point early warning, cascading risks from ecological to political systems, and the weaponisation of natural assets by states and corporations.

Published every Saturday at 06:00 BST.

## What This Monitor Tracks

- **Planetary Boundaries:** Weekly status across all nine Earth system processes defined by the Stockholm Resilience Centre / Potsdam Institute framework
- **Tipping Points:** Early warning tracking for AMOC, Amazon dieback, polar ice sheets, permafrost methane, and coral reef collapse
- **Cascading Risks:** Physical → human → political cascade chains across Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, MENA, Latin America, and the Arctic
- **Extreme Weather & Polycrisis:** Events assessed for inter-systemic feedback, not listed in isolation
- **Policy & Law:** UNFCCC/NDC compliance, ICJ Climate Advisory Opinion (standing tracker), Loss & Damage Finance Mechanism disbursement (standing tracker), climate litigation
- **AI & Climate Technology:** Dual-edge tracking of AI as both climate solution and environmental cost
- **Biosphere & Oceans:** Biodiversity Intactness Index, ocean heat content, marine biosphere integrity
- **Geostrategic Resource Competition:** Critical minerals, transboundary water rights, Arctic governance, natural capital as diplomatic leverage

## Analytical Position

Environmental degradation is treated as a structural condition that reshapes the strategic operating environment — not as a moral cause. The analytical register is identical to the [European Geopolitical & Hybrid Threat Monitor](/monitors/european-strategic-autonomy/) and the [World Democracy Monitor](/monitors/democratic-integrity/).
