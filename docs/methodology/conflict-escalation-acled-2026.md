## 3. Indicator Upgrades — I1 to I6

### I1 Rhetoric Intensity — AI-Generated Rhetoric Correction

**New failure mode (2026):** AI-generated propaganda now mimics official government rhetoric at scale. State-affiliated accounts producing AI-generated statements that superficially match official doctrine can inflate I1 scores if not screened.

**Correction protocol:**
- Before scoring any I1 item sourced from social media or unofficial channels, apply the **F1–F4 filter sequence** (already in methodology) with explicit note: *"AI generation check: does source have verifiable institutional origin?"*
- State-media and official government voice are indistinct for authoritarian actors (RU, CN, DPRK, Iran, Myanmar junta) — treat both as **T3 requiring T1/T2 corroboration** before scoring.
- Flag any I1 spike of ≥2 levels in a single week as requiring **dual-source confirmation** before committing to `persistent-state.json`.

### I2 Military Posture — Drone-Centric Doctrine Recognition

**New context (2026):** The Ukraine war has established drone swarms as a normalised tactical instrument rather than an escalation signal. FPV drone deployment, Shahed-136 equivalents, and naval drone operations are now baseline activity in several theatres. **Do not score routine drone operations at the same I2 level as the pre-2024 baseline.**

**Calibration additions:**
- **Drone baseline by theatre:** Ukraine/Russia: FPV and Shahed drone operations are baseline (do not score above I2 Level 2 for routine drone exchange). Sudan: drone use by RSF is a structural shift — score as I2 Level 3 if confirmed given the prior baseline.
- **New escalation threshold:** The relevant I2 escalation signal is now (a) **range extension** (e.g., long-range strike drones reaching previously non-targeted urban centres), (b) **swarm doctrine** (coordinated multi-vector simultaneous attack), or (c) **cross-border drone incursion** into a third-party state.
- **Satellite imagery verification:** Before scoring any major redeployment, check UNOSAT or Sentinel Hub EO Browser change detection. Distinguish between **exercise positions** (check NATO exercise calendar for NATO-proximate theatres) and **deployment anomaly**.

### I3 Nuclear/Strategic — Doctrine Shift vs. Rhetoric Distinction

**Critical 2026 context:** Russia's updated nuclear doctrine (November 2024) formally lowered the threshold for nuclear use in response to conventional attacks supported by nuclear states. This is a **confirmed Doctrine Shift** — it has already been coded. Do not re-code it as new each week.

**Correct application:**
- The November 2024 doctrine change is a **persistent baseline shift** for I3 in the Ukraine theatre, not a weekly event. Carry it in `persistent-state.json` as `doctrineshiftconfirmed: true`.
- Subsequent **rhetoric** referencing the doctrine (Medvedev statements, MFA warnings) is **episodic** — flag with `episodic: true`, do not increment I3 score.
- Only score a new I3 event when: (a) a second formal doctrine document is issued; (b) nuclear-capable delivery system is moved to **alert status** as confirmed by T1/T2 OSINT (Middlebury CNS, CSIS iDeas Lab); (c) IAEA safeguards access is formally suspended.
- **Israel ambiguity doctrine rule** (carried from extended notes): Israel's baseline I3 is structurally near-zero for public signalling. Any public Israeli nuclear signalling is automatically Level 4+ regardless of absolute language — it is anomalous by definition.

### I4 Economic Warfare — Tariff War Integration (NEW 2026)

**New structural condition:** The 2025–2026 US tariff escalation (effective tariff rate now 22%+ on Chinese imports; reciprocal tariff regimes active with EU, Canada, Mexico) has introduced **economic warfare instruments** into non-traditional theatres. This creates I4 scoring obligations for conflicts where economic coercion is a **secondary warfare instrument** rather than the primary conflict mode.

**New I4 scoring guidance:**
- Where a state is using tariff coercion, export controls, or sanctions **in conjunction with** kinetic or hybrid operations against the same target, score I4 as an **active indicator** even if the kinetic dimension is at a lower level.
- **DRC/mineral coercion:** US/EU critical mineral supply chain pressure intersecting with Rwandan proxy operations in Eastern DRC = I4 active. Cross-tag to GMM (commodity supply) and ERM (resource extraction conflict).
- **Russia energy cutoffs:** ACER EU energy regulator and IEA data are T1 for scoring energy coercion. Current status: Russian pipeline gas to EU is at residual levels post-2024 transit cutoff. I4 score for energy dimension is already at maximum for Russia–EU theatre; do not re-score weekly unless a new cutoff mechanism activates.

### I5 Diplomatic Channel Status — Multilateral Forum Tracking

**Clarification for 2026 diplomatic degradation context:**
- US withdrawal from 66+ multilateral institutions (per ESA monitor tracking) means that **US participation in multilateral forums** is no longer a reliable I5 positive indicator. A US presence at a UN forum does not carry the same de-escalation weight as in pre-2025 conditions.
- **New weighting rule:** For I5, weight **European, Gulf, and African Union mediation channels** more heavily than US bilateral channels in 2026. The US channel is currently structurally degraded as a de-escalation instrument in Gaza, Ukraine, and Sudan theatres.
- **Ambassador recall vs. expulsion coding rule** (from extended notes) remains mandatory: recall (actor-initiated, signalling) ≠ expulsion (host-country-initiated, higher escalation). Code them differently in all JSON entries.

### I6 Displacement Velocity — IDMC/IOM Source Protocol

**Source update:** The **IDMC Global Report on Internal Displacement 2025** (published May 2025) provides updated baseline figures for all active theatres. Load these figures into `persistent-state.json` as the displacement baseline for Q2 2026 calculations. Do not use 2023 IDMC figures as baseline for current displacement velocity calculations.

**Data date annotation (mandatory):** All I6 scores must include the `dataAsOf` field in the JSON entry. When data is more than **3 weeks behind** the scoring week, add `dataLagWarning: true` and note explicitly in the narrative.

**Rapid-onset vs. slow-onset distinction:** The following theatres have mixed rapid/slow onset displacement in 2026 — code the driver explicitly:
- **Sudan:** Rapid-onset (conflict) — SAF/RSF operations
- **Gaza:** Rapid-onset (kinetic war)
- **Pacific Islands / Bangladesh coastline:** Slow-onset (climate) — route to ERM not SCEM as primary
- **Sahel:** Mixed — conflict + climate drought = dual-driver, flag both SCEM and ERM

## 7. Failure Mode Corrections

### FM-SCEM-01: Drone Normalisation Inflation
**Problem:** Scoring routine drone exchanges at pre-2024 escalation thresholds inflates I2 scores in Ukraine, Gaza, and Sudan theatres.
**Correction:** Apply **theatre-specific drone baselines** as documented in I2 upgrades above. Document the baseline in `persistent-state.json` under each conflict's `indicatorBaselines.I2`.

### FM-SCEM-02: AI Rhetoric False Positive
**Problem:** AI-generated propaganda mimicking official statements inflates I1 scores.
**Correction:** All I1 items sourced below T2 require AI generation check before scoring. Add `aiGenerationCheck: true` field to I1 scoring entry when the check was applied.

### FM-SCEM-03: US Diplomatic Channel Over-weighting
**Problem:** Treating US bilateral engagement as structurally equivalent to pre-2025 de-escalation weight.
**Correction:** Apply reduced weighting to US-only diplomatic channels per I5 upgrade above. Document weighting rationale in narrative.

### FM-SCEM-04: Contested Baseline Impatience
**Carried from methodology — repeat for emphasis:** Entries remain CONTESTED for 1–3 weeks. Do not move to Confirmed because subsequent reporting feels consistent. The threshold exists because consistency over time distinguishes structural change from episodic noise.

## 8. Identity Card Supplement — 2026 Professional Standard

> The best conflict escalation analyst in 2026 understands that the operational environment has changed structurally in three ways that most conflict monitoring frameworks have not yet fully absorbed: (1) drone warfare is now baseline in multiple theatres, not an escalation marker; (2) AI-generated OSINT and fabricated battlefield reporting are now adversarial tools deployed by all major actors; (3) economic warfare instruments (tariffs, export controls, sanctions) are now routinely combined with kinetic and hybrid operations in ways that require joint SCEM/GMM analytical treatment.

> The failure mode to guard against most carefully in 2026 is **false precision** — applying the I1–I6 scoring rubric with apparent exactitude to situations where the underlying data is adversarially contaminated. When in doubt, hold the score and flag the uncertainty. A contested score that accurately represents epistemic uncertainty is more valuable than a false-precise score that projects analytical confidence the evidence does not support.
