ANALYTICAL TASKS:

A. REGIME ASSESSMENT REVIEW
Evaluate whether the current regime call (from conviction_history) is still
supported by the evidence:
- Compare the latest conviction_history entry against new weekly and daily data
- Check SA (Strategic Anchor) vs TS (Tactical Signal) indicator alignment —

- Flag if regime label should change (e.g. STAGFLATION→RECESSION)
- Apply the three-indicator corroboration test: no regime change on fewer
  than 3 corroborating indicators

B. ASSET CLASS DEVIATION ANALYSIS
For each of the 8 asset class baselines, assess:
- Whether the current_score has deviated significantly from baseline_score
- Whether the direction (Deteriorating/Improving/Stable) is still correct
  given new weekly and daily data

  blind_spot_overrides) — if so, annotate, don't suppress
- Flag asset classes where weekly_asset_class_summary diverges from the
  persistent baseline assessment

C. INDICATOR PATTERN ANALYSIS
From the daily Collector findings and weekly developments:
- Identify which findings are SA, CC, or TS type indicators
- Flag any TS spike that is NOT corroborated by SA/CC movement
  (apply episodic_flag: true)
- Flag any SA indicator movement that the current regime call doesn't reflect
- Identify US vs non-US divergence — are US-sourced indicators telling a
  different story than non-US data?

D. TAIL RISK ASSESSMENT
Review for emerging tail risks not yet captured in the regime call:
- Tariff escalation protocol: has the rung changed? Are new retaliators active?
- Oil supply shock: has the driver changed or resolved?
- Cross-monitor signals: do SCEM/ERM/ESA flags imply macro consequences
  not yet reflected in asset scores?
- Identify at least one non-consensus tail risk (something not in every
  sell-side deck)

E. CROSS-MONITOR FLAGS
Identify findings that should be flagged to adjacent monitors:
- SCEM: macro stress that could fuel conflict financing risk or political
  instability (sovereign stress → conflict risk multiplier)
- ESA: European macro conditions, EUR asset class signals, ECB policy divergence
- ERM: commodity chain stress, food price signals, energy supply disruption
- AGM: AI investment cycle signals, venture/private credit conditions
- WDM: economic stress as democratic backsliding accelerant

F. BLIND SPOT AUDIT
Check each active blind_spot_override against current data:
- Is the override still active / still relevant?
- Has the known false-positive pattern manifested this week?
- Should any new blind spot override be activated?

Do not cite any claim from the input data as `key_evidence` or elevate it to a `lead_signal` unless the `source_url` field for that claim is a verifiable primary source (a direct article, official publication, or institutional report URL — not a search result page, homepage, or aggregator).
