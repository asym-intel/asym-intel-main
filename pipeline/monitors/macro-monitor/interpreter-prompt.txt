## CONFIDENCE TIER STANDARD (cross-monitor canonical — v1.0, approved 16 Apr 2026)

Four tiers apply to all main signal confidence fields and key_judgments across all monitors.
Use exactly these labels — no synonyms.

| Tier | Label | Corroboration standard |
|------|-------|----------------------|
| 1 | Confirmed | 2+ independent sources, at least one T1-T2. No active contradicting evidence. Will be rare on this monitor — that is correct. |
| 2 | High | Single strong T1-T3 source, or 2+ T3-T4 sources consistent. No direct contradiction. |
| 3 | Assessed | Credible T3-T4 evidence. Analytical judgment supported but not independently corroborated. Default for most signals. |
| 4 | Possible | Min 2 independent source categories. Pattern-consistent but evidence thin, indirect, or early. |

key_judgments use three tiers only: High | Assessed | Possible
(Key judgments are analytical inferences — Confirmed is reserved for primary signal items.)

Auto-downgrade rule: if confidence == "Confirmed" but research_traceback lacks a T1-T2 source, downgrade to "High".
Cross-monitor flags use a separate scale: Low | Medium | High (unchanged).

Note on GMM: "Confirmed" is available on primary signal items when evidence meets the T1-T2 corroboration standard (e.g. central bank policy decisions, official IMF/BIS data releases).
It will be rare given the nature of macroeconomic signal data — that is analytically correct, not a gap.

## RULES

1. All 12 modules (module_0 through module_11) MUST appear in the output. Do not omit any.
2. lead_signal is the canonical top-of-brief anchor (headline, domain, body, source_url, confidence_preliminary). module_0 (The Signal) mirrors lead_signal for the renderer — write a punchy title and analytical body consistent with lead_signal. Both MUST be populated.
3. module_1 (Executive Insight) separates mainstream consensus from underweighted developments. Aim for 3-5 mainstream and 2-3 underweighted items.
4. module_2 (Policy Announcements and Data Releases) tracks central bank decisions, multilateral institution releases, and key data publications. Set flag=true for items with unverified or single-source reporting.
5. module_3 through module_6 cover Capital Flows, Indicator Domains, Currency and Sovereign Debt, and Institutional Research respectively. Populate richly.
6. module_7 (Risk Indicators) is a heat grid — rate each vector HIGH/ELEVATED/MODERATE/LOW. Set changed=true if the rating changed this issue. Include at least 6 risk vectors from the GMM domain: Policy Rate Divergence, Sovereign Spread Widening, Currency Regime Stress, Private Credit Cascade, Liquidity Fragmentation, Commodity Price Transmission, Banking Sector Stress, Trade Policy Shock.
7. module_8 through module_11 cover Financial System Stress, Trade and Tariff Policy, Macroeconomic Governance, and Scenario and Tail Risk.
8. delta_strip: the top 5 developments this issue, ranked by significance. Each links to its source module.
9. jurisdiction_risk_matrix: cover at least the US, EU, China, Japan, and 2-3 EM jurisdictions under acute stress.
10. central_bank_scorecard: cover at least the Fed, ECB, PBOC, BoE, and BoJ.
11. macro_health_composite: a 0-1 composite score with 5 sub-components. Be precise with numeric scores.
12. Stress regime is the primary organising framework. All developments map to Green | Amber | Red | Crisis where relevant.
13. A regime change requires evidence across at least two indicator domains — flag this discipline in module_4.
14. Tariff escalation: track the T1-T5 escalation rung in module_9. Update the rung assessment each cycle.
15. Central bank divergence: flag explicitly when two or more major central banks are moving in divergent directions.
16. Cross-monitor slugs: use full slugs as shown in schema above.
17. Do not search the web. Reason only over provided documents.
18. JSON string values must never contain apostrophes or contractions. Use full words only.
19. Every source_url field should contain a real URL from the source documents. Do not fabricate URLs.
20. If a module has no material this week, include it with empty arrays. Do not omit the key.
21. structured_claims[]: MUST contain one entry for every factual assertion in modules 0-11, delta_strip, jurisdiction_risk_matrix, central_bank_scorecard, macro_health_composite, and key_judgments. Deterministic extraction where the source field is already structured (regime rating, numeric score, policy rate). Use LLM-fallback (your own judgment) only for prose-only claims inside module_N.body fields. Every claim must carry evidence_id tracing to weekly-research or KB.
22. _meta.cycle_disposition: set to "material_change" if at least one module has new-this-cycle content; "null_cycle" if the evidence bundle carries no material change under the methodology rules; "partial_cycle" if mixed. Do NOT set "error_cycle" yourself — that is reserved for engine-level fallback paths.
22a. WEEKLY IS THE MATERIAL ANCHOR. The WEEKLY RESEARCH bundle (weekly-latest.json) is the primary source of material for cycle_disposition. Daily findings (verified-*.json) and reasoner analysis (reasoner-latest.json) are SUPPLEMENTARY context — they enrich the interpretation but do NOT override the weekly signal. Specifically:
    (a) If the weekly bundle contains new-this-cycle content (new policy rate decisions, sovereign spread moves, regime shifts, capital flow reversals, tariff escalation events, etc.), the cycle is material_change EVEN IF daily findings are zero and reasoner reports stable postures. Dailies are frequently thin in quiet-news periods; this does not make the weekly null.
    (b) null_cycle requires the WEEKLY bundle to be thin or absent OR carrying no material under the methodology rules. Quiet daily + stable reasoner alone are NEVER sufficient grounds for null_cycle when the weekly carries material.
    (c) When in doubt between material_change and null_cycle, err toward material_change if the weekly has 3+ new developments across any domains. The downstream Reviewer + Curator handle materiality gating; the Interpreter should not pre-emptively null-cycle substantive weekly content.
23. _meta.cycle_disposition_reason: one-sentence justification for the disposition value. Required if disposition is null_cycle or partial_cycle; optional otherwise.
24. No weekly_brief_draft: do NOT emit a weekly_brief_draft field. The Composer stage renders prose separately.
25. Claim-module consistency: if a claim appears in structured_claims[], its source_module reference must point to a module or field that actually contains the claim. Never credit a claim to a module that does not surface it.
26. gaps_register[] is optional but recommended when Possible confidence dominates a module — name the evidence that would upgrade.
27. proposed_patches[] is an OPTIONAL top-level array that names the material-change patches this interpretation wants the Applier to apply to the living knowledge base. Follow pipeline-doctrine Appendix A. Each patch MUST contain five fields: (a) rule_applied — the methodology rule you invoked (short string); (b) evidence_ids — array of evidence IDs from the weekly-research bundle or KB that support the patch (must be non-empty); (c) confidence — one of {"Confirmed","High","Assessed","Possible"}; (d) material_change_justification — one-sentence rationale for why this change is material; (e) target_kb_path — the KB path being updated (e.g. "module_7.vectors[sovereign_spread_widening].rating" or "module_4.domains[currency_fx].stress_level"). NULL-CYCLE CONTRACT: if the evidence bundle carries no material change under the methodology rules, emit proposed_patches: [] (empty array), not an omitted field. Never manufacture a patch under a null cycle.
