## CONFIDENCE TIER STANDARD (cross-monitor canonical — v1.0, approved 16 Apr 2026)

Four tiers apply to all main signal confidence fields and key_judgments across all monitors.
Use exactly these labels — no synonyms.

| Tier | Label | Corroboration standard |
|------|-------|----------------------|
| 1 | Confirmed | 2+ independent sources, at least one T1-T2. No active contradicting evidence. |
| 2 | High | Single strong T1-T3 source, or 2+ T3-T4 sources consistent. No direct contradiction. |
| 3 | Assessed | Credible T3-T4 evidence. Analytical judgment supported but not independently corroborated. Default for most signals. |
| 4 | Possible | Min 2 independent source categories. Pattern-consistent but evidence thin, indirect, or early. |

key_judgments use three tiers only: High | Assessed | Possible
(Key judgments are analytical inferences — Confirmed is reserved for primary signal items.)

Auto-downgrade rule: if confidence == "Confirmed" but research_traceback lacks a T1-T2 source, downgrade to "High".
Cross-monitor flags use a separate scale: Low | Medium | High (unchanged).

## ANALYTICAL FILTERS (F1–F4)

Apply each filter when triggered by findings.

- **F1 — State Capture Filter.** Triggered when the distinction between state and private criminal interest has collapsed. Requires explicit judgment on whether the state is directing the financial architecture or has been captured by it. Cross-reference with WDM.

- **F2 — Sanctions Architecture Filter.** Triggered by any documented sanctions-evasion scheme. Requires three-level analysis: (a) the scheme itself, (b) the enabling architecture (jurisdictions, intermediaries, structures), (c) the strategic consequence.

- **F3 — Enabler Jurisdiction Filter.** Triggered when a jurisdiction's legal or regulatory framework facilitates illicit flows. Requires four-dimension assessment: (a) legal framework design, (b) enforcement reality, (c) capacity deficit vs. political choice, (d) systemic significance.

- **F4 — Conflict Finance Filter.** Triggered by any financial flow funding, sustaining, or profiting from armed conflict. Requires three-stage trace: source → channel → deployment. Cross-reference SCEM for conflict context and ERM for commodity-flow data.

## STRUCTURED CLAIMS FORMAT

For every factual assertion, emit a structured_claims[] entry:

```json
{
  "claim_id": "fim-YYYY-WNN-NNN",
  "entity": "entity name (jurisdiction, organisation, person, mechanism)",
  "predicate": "what is asserted about the entity",
  "value": "the specific value, direction, or state",
  "evidence_id": "source identifier (url, source_name, or document reference)",
  "confidence": "Confirmed | High | Assessed | Possible",
  "source_module": "weekly_research | reasoner.<section> | persistent.<module> | daily.<module>",
  "notes": "short provenance/interpretation note explaining why this assertion matters",
  "domain": "D1 | D2 | D3 | D4 | D5",
  "pillar": "AML | CTF | CPF | cross-pillar",
  "filters_applied": ["F1", "F2", "F3", "F4"]
}
```

`source_module` and `notes` are mandatory data-contract fields. The Reviewer
R3 gate will hold publication if any structured_claims[] item omits them.

Minimum 5 structured_claims[] on a material-change cycle. Zero is acceptable only on null_cycle.
