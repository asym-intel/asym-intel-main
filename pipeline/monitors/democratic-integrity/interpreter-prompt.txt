## CONFIDENCE TIER STANDARD (cross-monitor canonical — v1.0, approved 16 Apr 2026)

Four tiers apply to all main signal confidence fields and key_judgments across all monitors.
Use exactly these labels — no synonyms.

| Tier | Label | Corroboration standard |
|------|-------|----------------------|
| 1 | Confirmed | 2+ independent sources, at least one T1-T2. No active contradicting evidence. Will be rare on this monitor — that is correct. |
| 2 | High | Single strong T1-T3 source, or 2+ T3-T4 sources consistent. No direct contradiction. |
| 3 | Assessed | Credible T3-T4 evidence. Analytical judgment supported but not independently corroborated. Default for most signals. |
| 4 | Possible | Min 2 independent source categories. Pattern-consistent but evidence thin, indirect, or early. |

key_judgments use three tiers only: High | Assessed | Possible
(Key judgments are analytical inferences — Confirmed is reserved for primary signal items.)

Auto-downgrade rule: if confidence == "Confirmed" but research_traceback lacks a T1-T2 source, downgrade to "High".
Cross-monitor flags use a separate scale: Low | Medium | High (unchanged).

Note on WDM: "Confirmed" is available on primary signal items when evidence meets the T1-T2 corroboration standard.
It will be rare given the nature of electoral integrity data — that is analytically correct, not a gap.

## RULES

1. All 16 modules (module_0 through module_15) MUST appear in the output. Do not omit any.
2. lead_signal is the canonical top-of-brief anchor (headline, body, source_url, confidence_preliminary). module_0 (The Signal) mirrors lead_signal for the renderer — write a factual title and analytical body consistent with lead_signal. Both MUST be populated.
3. module_1 (Executive Insight) separates mainstream consensus from underweighted developments. Aim for 3-5 mainstream and 2-3 underweighted items.
4. module_2 (Election-Cycle Events) tracks elections, electoral calendar announcements, and election-cycle developments. Flag items where the source is unverified.
5. module_3 through module_6 cover Power Concentration, Electoral Environment, Regional Watch, and Civil Society Research respectively. Populate richly.
6. module_7 (Risk Indicators) is a heat grid — rate each vector HIGH/ELEVATED/MODERATE/LOW. Set changed=true if the rating changed this issue. Include at least 6 risk vectors covering: Electoral Administration Capture, Judicial Independence Erosion, Power Concentration, Mimicry Chain Acceleration, FIMI Electoral Exposure, Civil Society Space Contraction.
7. module_8 through module_15 cover Electoral Violence, Legal Watch, Electoral Integrity Governance, Media Freedom, International Standards, Democratic Backsliding and Society, Power Structures, and Personnel.
8. delta_strip: the top 5 developments this issue, ranked by significance. Each links to its source module.
9. jurisdiction_risk_matrix: cover at least EU member states in acute backsliding, US, key global south cases, and 2-3 other jurisdictions.
10. institutional_health_scorecard: cover the 6-8 most significant electoral management bodies, constitutional courts, or institutional actors this week.
11. democracy_health_composite: a 0-1 composite score with 5 sub-components. Be precise with numeric scores.
12. V-Dem framework is primary organising framework. All country developments map to Electoral/Liberal/Participatory/Deliberative/Egalitarian components where relevant.
13. Electoral FIMI: flag FIMI incidents in module_5 (global_south.fimi_electoral) and module_9 (standards_highlights); never attribute interference based on circumstantial evidence alone.
14. Venice Commission and OSCE ODIHR standards: track implementation and compliance in module_5 (european) and module_12.
15. Transnational repression: flag in module_8 and cross-reference in module_5 if relevant.
16. Cross-monitor slugs: use full slugs as shown in schema above.
17. Do not search the web. Reason only over provided documents.
18. JSON string values must never contain apostrophes or contractions. Use full words only.
19. Every source_url field should contain a real URL from the source documents. Do not fabricate URLs.
20. If a module has no material this week, include it with empty arrays. Do not omit the key.
