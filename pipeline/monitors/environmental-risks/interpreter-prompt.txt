## CONFIDENCE TIER STANDARD (cross-monitor canonical — v1.0, approved 16 Apr 2026)

Four tiers apply to all main signal confidence fields and key_judgments across all monitors.
Use exactly these labels — no synonyms.

| Tier | Label | Corroboration standard |
|------|-------|----------------------|
| 1 | Confirmed | 2+ independent sources, at least one T1-T2. No active contradicting evidence. Will be rare on this monitor — that is correct. |
| 2 | High | Single strong T1-T3 source, or 2+ T3-T4 sources consistent. No direct contradiction. |
| 3 | Assessed | Credible T3-T4 evidence. Analytical judgment supported but not independently corroborated. Default for most signals. |
| 4 | Possible | Min 2 independent source categories. Pattern-consistent but evidence thin, indirect, or early. |

key_judgments use three tiers only: High | Assessed | Possible
(Key judgments are analytical inferences — Confirmed is reserved for primary signal items.)

Auto-downgrade rule: if confidence == "Confirmed" but research_traceback lacks a T1-T2 source,
downgrade to "High".
Cross-monitor flag urgency uses a separate uppercase scale: HIGH | MEDIUM | LOW.

Note on ERM: primary data sources (Copernicus C3S, IPCC, IPBES, WMO) are Tier 1.
Findings backed by these sources are eligible for "Confirmed" when independently corroborated.
Tipping point proximity assessments are typically "Assessed" or "Possible" — they are modelled
projections, not direct measurements.

## ANALYTICAL FILTERS (apply to every item)

### Filter 1 — The Threat Multiplier
Applied to: climate_security_nexus, extreme_events_log
Trigger: Environmental event acting as vector to disrupt a secondary human system.
Action: Flag the transition from ecological basin to geopolitical/economic basin.
The cascade chain must be explicitly traced: physical to human to political.
Reverse Cascade Check: Does a geopolitical event accelerate an Earth-system boundary transgression?

### Filter 2 — The Regulatory Vacuum
Applied to: policy_law_compliance, loss_damage_tracker
Trigger: Gap between stated environmental ambitions and actual enforcement.
Action: Flag whether responses are reactive/management-oriented vs. transformative.
Identify root cause: political capture, austerity, or geopolitical realignment.
F2 vs F4 decision: F2 = mechanism exists but unenforced; F4 = no binding mechanism exists at all.

### Filter 3 — Tipping Point Precursor
Applied to: planetary_boundary_tracker, tipping_point_tracker
Trigger: Scientific indicator of shift from self-dampening to self-reinforcing feedback loop.
Action: Flag critical transitions across the six core tipping systems.
AMOC-specific: Track FovS (freshwater flux at 34S) as lead indicator.

### Filter 4 — The Attribution Gap
Applied to: planetary_boundary_tracker, attribution_gap_cases
Trigger: Environmental harm with no binding international governance mechanism.
Action: Flag governance void — what Filter 2 misses (total absence vs. non-enforcement).
Primary cases: Novel Entities (Synthetic Chemicals), Deep-Sea Mining (ISA Governance Void),
Atmospheric Aerosol Loading (Shipping Fuel Transition), Coral Reef Bleaching (No International
Liability Framework).
