(EU terminology for state-sponsored disinformation). It does NOT refer to the Malaysian

return film-industry content, discard those results and re-query with:

RESEARCH SCOPE — search for content from these sources and organisations:
Tier 1 (platform disclosures + government attribution):
  - Meta coordinated inauthentic behaviour (CIB) reports and transparency disclosures
  - Google Threat Analysis Group (TAG) reports
  - Microsoft MSTIC threat intelligence blog

  - US State Department Global Engagement Center statements

Tier 2 (academic + institutional):
  - Stanford Internet Observatory research and reports
  - EU DisinfoLab publications
  - EDMO (European Digital Media Observatory) bulletins

Tier 3 (reputable OSINT):
  - DFRLab (Atlantic Council) reporting
  - ASPI International Cyber Policy Centre
  - Bellingcat investigations

SIX ACTORS — apply IDENTICAL evidentiary standards to all:
Russia, China, Iran, Gulf states, United States, Israel

ANALYTICAL FILTERS — apply to every finding before including:
MF1: Is this story itself a FIMI operation? (meta-disinfo check)
MF2: Is actor attribution based on single source? (flag if yes)
MF3: Is the claim independently verifiable? (flag if no)
MF4: Is primary source state media? (flag if yes)

CRITICAL RULES:
- Coordination evidence ≠ state attribution. Never collapse them.
- X/Twitter has no CIB equivalent — absence of disclosure ≠ absence of activity
- Gulf/US/Israel will often have lower confidence due to structural OSINT gap — this is correct
- Focus on NEW developments this week — not background or historical material
- If no new developments are found across all Tier 1–3 sources after exhaustive search,
  return status: "quiet-week" in _meta and populate weekly_brief_narrative with a

  contradictory. A quiet week lead_signal should carry confidence_preliminary: "N/A"
  and a headline of "No new FIMI operations identified this week — see coverage notes."
